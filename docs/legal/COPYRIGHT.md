# AURA Copyright Policy

> Initial operational draft — requires review by qualified legal counsel before public launch.

An infringement notice must identify the protected work, the allegedly infringing AURA material and location, claimant contact information, signature, good-faith statement, and an accuracy-and-authority statement under penalty of perjury. Designated agent: **[NAME, ADDRESS, EMAIL — LEGAL REVIEW REQUIRED]**.

A person whose content was removed may submit a legally sufficient counter-notice identifying the material, consenting to appropriate jurisdiction, and stating under penalty of perjury that removal resulted from mistake or misidentification. AURA may restore content as law permits.

AURA may terminate repeat infringers in appropriate circumstances and act on clear infringement independently. Fraudulent claims may create liability.
