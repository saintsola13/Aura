# AURA Privacy Policy

> Initial operational draft — requires review by qualified legal counsel before public launch.

Last updated: July 20, 2026

AURA processes verified emails; immutable X subject IDs and current basic X profile details; AURA account and profile data; manually entered public wallet addresses; posts, media, follows and engagement; session cookies and device summaries; shortened or secret-peppered network identifiers; and security events. Public profiles, posts, media, and wallet references are public.

We use data to authenticate, provide the social service, communicate transactional messages, prevent abuse, secure sessions, moderate content, comply with law, and improve reliability. Wallet references are never authentication or ownership proof.

Cloudflare provides network, Workers, D1, R2, Turnstile, and security processing. Resend delivers transactional email. X supplies identity information when a person chooses Continue with X. These providers may process data internationally under their terms and safeguards.

AURA uses HttpOnly session cookies and a CSRF cookie; hashes session tokens before storage; and does not intentionally retain raw OAuth codes, X access tokens, or raw magic-link tokens after their purpose. Security records are retained for proportionate periods. Active account data is retained while needed for the service, security, disputes, law, and backups.

Deletion has a 30-day cancellation period followed by deletion or anonymization under operational policy. AURA cannot delete public blockchain data. AURA is not directed to children under the applicable digital-consent age. Material changes will be posted and, where appropriate, notified in-product or by email.
